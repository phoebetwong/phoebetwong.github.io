Welcome to my website!

I'm a PhD Candidate at American University Department of Economics. My dissertation focuses on how special interest influence affects fiscal policies.

In Sep 2019, I completed the Data Science Fellowship at Flatiron School DC, where I learned the latest and greatest machine learning and deep learning models with Python. I also went outside the standard data science curriculum and learned how to implement machine learning techniques to make causal inferences, specifically using several models built by Susan Athey at Stanford in one of the chapters of my dissertation.

I spent the last 2+ years building and running a nonprofit called Equal Citizens with Prof. Lawrence Lessig at Harvard Law School. Equal Citizens advances democracy reform in the U.S. through a variety of projects including election reform litigations, grassroot mobilization, and education campaigns. One of our cases will most likely be reviewed by the Supreme Court before the 2020 election!

Previously, I was a senior economist at the Joint Economic Committee of the US Congress, where I advised Members of Congress and staffers on macroeconomic, fiscal, and monetary policy issues.

Before that, I was a consultant at the World Bank, where I contributed to macro, fiscal, and economic development policy research in the Europe and Central Asia and Latin America regions.

Any views expressed here are my own and do not represent the views of my past or current employers.

Please feel free to contact me via LinkedIn or Medium.
